Email & Security

Whether you’re just establishing your email accounts, moving them, or trying to harden them against intrusion, our comprehensive solutions focus on securing your relied-on communications systems. Today, 9 of every 10 malware attacks happen through email. Email is increasingly the front door for cyber attacks. Intellium provides forward-thinking solutions to meet your business email needs while elevating the security of those options.

  • Email migrations
  • Deliverability issues
  • Email rules
  • Email encryption
  • Troubleshooting
  • Email troubleshooting

  • Email security, including:

    • Sender Policy Framework (SPF) – establishing protections for which IP addresses are authorized to email on behalf of your domain, to reduce fraud and spoofing
    • DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM) – using cryptography to sign your messages, to verify email integrity and assure the contents, including attachments, haven’t been altered
    • Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance (DMARC) – guiding receiving mail systems in how to manage messages sent from your domain that fail SPF or DKIM checks

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the hot topic today for a reason: technology attacks take place with alarming regularity, targeting major corporations, but also now medium or smaller-sized businesses and individuals. Cyberattacks are a looming threat for any organization, exploiting gaps as straightforward as a simple weakness in your email setup or as complex as a systems-wide faulty permission setup. Intellium focuses on the tactics that help prevent that.

The good news is that it’s not all doom and gloom. Research from Microsoft also indicates that two-factor identification (2FA) can make your account 99.9% less likely to be compromised. From equipping you with smarter technologies to assuring you are taking advantage of best practices for improved security, Intellium works with you to make cybersecurity a foundation of your business IT.

  • Encryption

  • HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) – helping protect websites against protocol downgrade attacks, cookie hijacking, and more
  • Automatic Certificate Management Environment (ACME) protocol
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) /Multi-Factor Authentication
  • Vulnerability Management
